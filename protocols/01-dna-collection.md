# Protocol 1: Buccal Cell Collection

**Time:** ~20 minutes active
**Prerequisites:** Centrifuge, 1.5mL tubes, saline solution
**Output:** Cell pellet in 1.5mL tube (~matchstick-head sized white dot)

---

## Prep (Before Starting)

- [ ] Prepare saline: dissolve ~0.3g table salt (small pinch) in 30–35 mL water
- [ ] Label a 1.5mL tube: "CELLS — [date]"
- [ ] Prepare counterbalance tube: 1.5mL tube filled with 1 mL water
- [ ] Do NOT eat, drink (except water), or brush teeth for 30 min before

---

## Step 1: Collect Buccal Cells

- [ ] Pour ~10 mL saline into mouth
- [ ] Swish vigorously for 60 seconds — rub cheeks against teeth
- [ ] Spit into a clean glass or bowl
- [ ] Transfer ~1.5 mL of the mouthwash into your labeled CELLS tube using P1000

---

## Step 2: Pellet Cells

- [ ] Place CELLS tube in centrifuge with hinge facing outward
- [ ] Place counterbalance tube (1 mL water) in the opposite position
- [ ] Spin at **4,000 × g for 3 minutes**
- [ ] Remove carefully — look for white pellet at bottom of tube
- [ ] Carefully pour off supernatant (clear liquid), leaving pellet + ~20–50 µL liquid
- [ ] Check pellet size — should be roughly a matchstick head

**If pellet is too small:** Collect another mouthwash sample, transfer to same tube, spin again.

---

## Step 3: Wash Pellet

- [ ] Add 1 mL fresh saline to the pellet tube
- [ ] Flick tube firmly to loosen pellet, pipette up/down with P1000 until uniformly cloudy
- [ ] Place in centrifuge with counterbalance (1 mL water tube)
- [ ] Spin at **4,000 × g for 3 minutes**
- [ ] Confirm pellet has reformed
- [ ] Pour off supernatant, leave pellet + ~50–100 µL liquid

---

## Timing Notes
- Once cells are pelleted, **proceed to lysis within 30 minutes** or refrigerate at 4°C
- Active nucleases in intact cells will degrade DNA if left too long
- Once lysis is complete (Protocol 02, Step 2), time pressure is relieved

---

## Troubleshooting

| Problem | Likely cause | Fix |
|---------|-------------|-----|
| No visible pellet | Didn't swish hard enough, or centrifuge too slow | Collect fresh sample, swish harder |
| Pellet very small | Low cell yield | Run a second collection and add to same tube |
| Supernatant still cloudy after spin | Speed too low, or tube unbalanced | Ensure counterbalance, verify speed setting |
