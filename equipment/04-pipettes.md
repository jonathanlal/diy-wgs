# Equipment: Micropipettes

**Decision status:** OPEN — leaning toward used mid-range set

---

## What They Do
Accurately transfer precise volumes of liquid (0.1 µL to 1000 µL).
Precision matters: wrong volumes in library prep can kill a flow cell run.

---

## Sizes Needed
| Size | Range | Used for |
|------|-------|---------|
| P2 | 0.1–2.5 µL | Small reagent additions (Rapid kit: 1 µL FRA) |
| P20 | 2–20 µL | DNA aliquots, small transfers |
| P200 | 20–200 µL | Most extraction steps |
| P1000 | 100–1000 µL | Large volume transfers, cell washes |

P2 is the trickiest — 1 µL volumes require a good pipette. Don't skimp on this one.

---

## Options

### 1. New Budget Pipettes (off-brand) — CHEAP BUT RISKY
Brands like CAPP, Eppendorf Xplorer knockoffs, Dragon Lab on Amazon:
- Full set of 4: ~$80–150
- Accuracy is variable — some are fine, some drift
- No calibration certificates
- Risk: miscalibrated P2 = wrong reagent ratios = bad library = dead flow cell

### 2. Used Name-Brand Pipettes — RECOMMENDED
Eppendorf Research Plus, Gilson Pipetman, Rainin Classic on eBay:
- Individual used pipettes: ~$30–60 each
- Full set of 4 used: ~$120–200
- These are lab-grade, well-documented, can be serviced and recalibrated
- Gilson Pipetman is the gold standard — extremely common used

**What to buy:**
- Gilson Pipetman P2, P20, P200, P1000 — used set on eBay: ~$120–180
- Or Eppendorf Research Plus set — similar price range

### 3. New Name-Brand Pipettes — PREMIUM
- Gilson Pipetman Classic set of 4: ~$400–600 new
- Eppendorf Research Plus set: ~$400–500 new
- Overkill for our use case, but these last decades

### 4. Electronic Pipettes — NOT NEEDED
- Improve ergonomics for repetitive work
- $300–600 each
- Not worth it for occasional home use

### 5. DIY Pipette — NOT RECOMMENDED
- Calibrated liquid transfer at µL scale is genuinely hard to DIY
- Syringe + tubing approaches exist but accuracy at 1 µL is very poor
- The cost savings vs. a used set are minimal

---

## Tips (Consumables)
Tips must match the pipette brand for accurate volume delivery.
- Gilson Pipetman uses "Diamond Tips" (Gilson-branded) or universal tips
- Universal filtered tips work fine (filtered = prevent aerosol contamination)
- Buy filtered tips — prevents cross-contamination between samples

**Buy 3 box sizes:**
- 10 µL tips (for P2/P20): ~$20/box of 96
- 200 µL tips (for P200): ~$20/box of 96
- 1000 µL tips (for P1000): ~$20/box of 96

You burn through tips fast — buy 2–3 boxes of each before starting.

---

## Verdict
**Buy used Gilson Pipetman set (P2, P20, P200, P1000) on eBay. ~$120–180.**
Don't go full budget/off-brand — the P2 especially needs to be accurate.
Add filtered tips in all three sizes.
